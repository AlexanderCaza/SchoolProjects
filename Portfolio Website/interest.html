Investing

One of the things that I love learning about, researching and analyzing is the stock market and investments in general. One of my favourite movies is The Big Short. My parents didn't have a problem discussing money and economics with me. They also both have MBAs. My father often sits down and watches the Bloomberg television channel. There have been many times when my father and I have gotten into a discussion about the stock market or economics at 10 PM and we only notice after midnight that we've been talking for over 2 hours. I find the topic of investing absolutely fascinating. My favourite billionaire in the world is probably Warren Buffett, the chairman and CEO of Berkshire Hathaway. I have always been the kind of child who saved the money he got as opposed to spending it like most children would. I cannot remember a time when I was in elementary when I spent my money to buy candy or toys when I was out somewhere. Over the years, I've built up savings of about $6000. My parents are very supportive of my interest in investing and have just finished setting up an in-trust account for me so that I can have my father buy stocks for me through it. He said he'll let me dictate which stocks I plan. I'm super excited to begin purchasing stocks once the stock market bottoms out due to the rising interest rates and the possible recession that may occur. I am very glad that the markets are crashing as it will give me an opportunity to enter the markets at the bottom and make money off of their recovery.

My Top Stock Picks

Once the stock market has finished falling due to rising interest rates and the recession that may occur, I have a number of stocks that I have have found that I plan to buy. Here are some of the stocks I plan to buy:

Lions Gate Entertainment Corp. Cl B (NYSE: LGF.B)

In my opinion, Lions Gate is significantly undervalued with a market cap of only 2 billion dollars. They are also currently working on producing a new Hunger Games movie called The Ballad of Songbirds and Snakes which is set to be released November 17, 2023. The 4 previous Hunger Games movies collectively grossed over 3 billion dollars int he box office. Lions gate is also the distribution Company for the upcoming John Wick: Chapter 4 movie which releases March 24, 2023. The Third John Wick movie grossed over 327 million dollars in the box office. Historically, when Lions Gate produces a blockbuster such as the original Hunger Games movies, their stock price shoots up before it releases and stays up after it releases for a time. Lions also usually pay dividends after it released a blockbuster movie. As such, I expect the stock price of Lions Gate to increase significantly in the lead up to the release of The Ballad of Songbirds and Snakes and so I plan to buy some stock in it before the stock price begins going up.

Microsoft Corporation (NASDAQ: MSFT)

I believe Microsoft has room to grow. I believe it's CEO Satya Nadella is an excellent CEO who is working to grow Microsoft's business. In my opinion, Microsoft is one of the leader's in the cloud computing industry. I believe this industry will grow hugely in the next decade. I also like that Microsoft has a fairly diverse list of products and subsidiaries. They have OneDrive which does cloud storage and who's business I think will grow greatly over time. They also own companies like Github and even Flipgrid.

Berkshire Hathaway Inc. Class B (NYSE: BRK.B)

As stated previously, I am a huge fan of Warren Buffett. I really like his investing style and I think hes a brilliant investor. Berkshire Hathaway is a conglomerate meaning that it owns a number of smaller companies. In my opinion, Berkshire Hathaway is significant undervalued. It's cap is below 600 billions dollars, while the companies and assets that it owns are collectively worth 959 billions dollars. In my opinion, the market cap of a company should at the very least match the total value of it's assets, especially in the case of a conglomerate. Berkshire Hathaway is also sitting on 149 billion dollars in cash and we are going into a bear market. This means that Berkshire Hathaway is in an excellent position to capitalise on the bear market and acquire some companies for great prices. Finally, Berkshire Hathaway has lately been making some excellent business decisions lately in my opinion. Berkshire Hathaway increased its stake in Chevron, an energy company from under 5 billion dollars at the end of 2021 to over 25 billion dollars at the end of March. With raising oil prices, this was an excellent business decision.
